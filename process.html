How Computer Forensics Works

The Process of Obtaining and Using Digital Evidence

Home | Digital Evidence | The Process

Computer forensics must be done carefully because digital evidence can be lost, changed, damaged, or made inaccessible. Investigators have to follow specific steps to guarantee that the evidence stays reliable and usable in court.

Steps in the Process

  1. Identify where the evidence might be located.
  2. Protect and preserve the evidence.
  3. Create a copy of the data if needed.
  4. Analyze the evidence.
  5. Keep a formal record of what was found.
  6. Present findings in court if necessary.

Why the Process Matters

Investigators have to be careful so they do not accidentally change or damage important information. In some cases, investigators create a copy of the data so the original evidence stays protected. Also, it is important that investigators stick to this process so that findings are able to be held in the court of law.

Step Purpose
