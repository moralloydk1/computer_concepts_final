How Computer Forensics Works

The process of protecting and analyzing digital evidence

Home Digital Evidence The Process

Step 1: Identify Evidence

Investigators must first identify where digital evidence might be located. This could include in a phone, laptop, email account, flash drive, hard drive, or another online platform.

Step 2: Preserve Evidence

Digital evidence can be changed or damaged easily, so investigators must protect it. They may create a copy of the data, called a bit-for-bit-copy, so the original evidence stays unaltered.

Step 3: Analyze and Report

After the evidence is secured, investigators must analyze the evidence obtained and keep a formal record of what they discover. If needed, the findings may be presented in court.
