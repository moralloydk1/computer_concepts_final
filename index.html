Digital Evidence

Information Stored or Shared Through Technology

Home | Digital Evidence | The Process

Digital evidence is any information that is stored or transmitted digitally and may be used in court. It can be found on phones, computers, hard drives, flash drives, cameras, cloud accounts, and online platforms.

Examples of Digital Evidence

  • Text messages
  • Call logs
  • Emails
  • Photos and videos
  • Browser history
  • Location records
  • Social media activity
  • Files as well as deleted files

How Digital Evidence Helps

Digital evidence can help investigators understand what fully happened in a case. For example, location records may show where a device was used, while text messages and emails may show who someone communicated with and what they talked about. Evidence collected has the potential to strengthen a case.

Digital Evidence Chart

Type of Evidence What It Can Show
Text Messages Who someone talked to
Location Records Where a device may have been
Browser History Websites visited on a device
