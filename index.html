What Is Computer Forensics?

Computer forensics, also called digital forensics, is the process of collecting, analyzing, and tracing digital evidence. This evidence can come from phones, laptops, emails, hard drives, cloud accounts, and other electronic devices.

Why It Matters

Now that technology is part of everyday life, investigations may now need digital evidence. Phones, computers, and tablets can reveal search history, emails, location records, call logs, text messages, and other information that has the potential to support a case.
