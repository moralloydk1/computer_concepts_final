How Deleted Data Is Recovered

When a person deletes a file, the file is not always completely erased from the computer right away. In many cases, the computer marks the space as available to be used again. Until new data replaces it, the deleted file may still be recoverable.

Why Deleted Files Can Still Exist

A deleted file may stay on a hard drive, flash drive, or phone storage even after it disappears from the folder. Computer forensic investigators use special tools to search for pieces of deleted data.

Steps Investigators May Follow

  1. Protect the original device from being changed.
  2. Create a copy of the device or storage drive.
  3. Use forensic software to search for deleted files.
  4. Study the recovered data carefully.
  5. Prepare a report explaining what was found.

Why This Matters

Deleted data can be important in investigations because it may show what happened on a device. However, investigators must follow rules so the evidence is handled legally and fairly.
